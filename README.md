# Staff-engineers-path

If you’re thinking without writing, you only think you’re thinking. - Leslie Lamport

This is my take onto prepare to be staff engineer. 

A staff engineer is basical senior software engineer++ with broader thinking and broader stakeholder management. 

This guide I will mostly talk about the technical side of it. What are the things you should know as a staff engineer. The path is different if you are prepping to be a staff by promotion and by switching companies. However, the skills are good to have. 


This guide is ideal for distributed system engineers. The guide will first prepare to crack the distributed system engineers interviews. Then we will go deeper to increase the knowledge base. 


#### The must for Interview
1. Datastructure and Algorithm
2. Low level design
3. System Design
4. Observability

I understand that the observability comes as the part of system design but I love observability and I think it gives extra edge for your staff engineer skills. 

We will go in deep on each concepts to do most in each of the sections.

##### Low Level Design

I think low level design comes naturally to the senior software engineers. However, you should know few questions to solve this. 

1. Design patterns 
    - https://refactoring.guru/design-patterns Design patterns by refactoring guru is a good resource. However I would suggest to use chatgpt to understand more. 

2. SOLID principals
    - This is must know. This helps a lot in the low level design

3. Try solving some real life lld questions. A course by educative on Grokking the Low Level Design Interview Using OOD Principles is very well curated. There are around 20 questions that can help sharpen the skill. I personally will highly recommend to atleast solve 10 questions. 

4. Domain driven design
5. Clean Architecture - Uncle bob martin
6. Onion-Architecture - https://jeffreypalermo.com/2008/07/the-onion-architecture-part-1/
